Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

Overview

In 1927, Werner Heisenberg introduced the Uncertainty Principle, a cornerstone of quantum mechanics. It states that it is impossible to simultaneously determine the exact position and momentum of a particle with absolute precision. This principle has profound implications for our understanding of the quantum world.
