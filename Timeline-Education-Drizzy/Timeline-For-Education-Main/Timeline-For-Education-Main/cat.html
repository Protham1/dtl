Schrödinger's Cat

Overview

In 1935, physicist Erwin Schrödinger proposed a thought experiment to illustrate the paradoxes of quantum mechanics. In the scenario, a cat is placed in a box with a radioactive atom that may or may not decay, triggering a mechanism that kills the cat. According to quantum theory, until the box is opened, the cat is simultaneously alive and dead. This paradox highlights the strange nature of quantum superposition and measurement.
