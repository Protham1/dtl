Bell's Theorem

Overview

In 1964, physicist John Bell formulated Bell's Theorem, which demonstrated that no physical theory of local hidden variables can ever reproduce all the predictions of quantum mechanics. The theorem shows that quantum entanglement, a phenomenon where particles become correlated in a way that defies classical physics, cannot be explained by any theory that assumes local realism. Bell's Theorem has profound implications for the nature of reality and the validity of quantum mechanics over classical interpretations.
