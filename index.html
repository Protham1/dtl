Timeline of Quantum Mechanics

Explore key discoveries and events in the field of quantum mechanics.

1900 - Planck's Quantum Hypothesis

Max Planck introduces the idea that energy is quantized, laying the foundation for quantum mechanics.

1905 - Einstein's Photoelectric Effect

Albert Einstein explains the photoelectric effect, demonstrating the particle nature of light.

1925 - Heisenberg's Matrix Mechanics

Werner Heisenberg formulates matrix mechanics, a cornerstone of quantum theory.

1926 - Schrödinger's Wave Equation

Erwin Schrödinger develops the wave equation to describe quantum states of particles.

1927 - Uncertainty Principle

Werner Heisenberg introduces the uncertainty principle, revealing limits to the precision of measurements.

1935 - Schrödinger's Cat Thought Experiment

Erwin Schrödinger illustrates quantum superposition and measurement issues with his famous cat paradox.

1964 - Bell's Theorem

John Bell formulates Bell's theorem, demonstrating the non-locality of quantum mechanics.
