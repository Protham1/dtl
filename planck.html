Planck's Quantum Hypothesis

Overview

In 1900, Max Planck introduced the idea that energy is quantized, which became a fundamental concept in quantum mechanics. Planck proposed that energy is emitted or absorbed in discrete units called "quanta." This idea was revolutionary and laid the groundwork for modern physics.
